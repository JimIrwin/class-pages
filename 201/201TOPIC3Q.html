* Advanced Questions on Demand -- these next 4 require more thought (and a fuller response) *

According to the law of demand, when the price of pencils increases people buy fewer pencils. Is this a better example of the income effect or the substitution effect ? Explain.

Suppose that 1/4 of all the cherries sold in Michigan are bought by Canadian companies, for resale in Canada. Explain (and draw) what would happen to the demand for Michigan cherries if the Canadian government outlawed the importation of cherries from Michigan.

True/False/Explain: If Sony offers buyers of their TVs a $100 per TV mail-in rebate then the demand for Sony TVs would shift upward by $100 per TV.

Suppose there is news that contrary to popular belief, eating carrots does not help a person's eyesight.
a. Use supply and demand analysis to explain how the carrot market would be affected.
b  Explain how carrot producers would be affected.
c. What other markets might be affected by the news ? (discuss).

 

Suppose that the population of Mt Pleasant increased (e.g. because CMU and the casino both expanded).

a. Use supply and demand analysis to discuss the effect on the market for rental housing (rooms, apartments, houses).
b. What do you think would happen to dorm rates at CMU ? Discuss (note that CMU is a price-maker in the market for dorm rooms).

 

Suppose international collaboration destroys the Mexican marijuana crop

a. Use the S&D model to show what happens to the market for pot in the US.

b. how are US pot consumers affected ?

c. how are non-Mexican pot producers affected ?

Suppose milk producers adopt a synthetic growth hormone that significantly increases the amount of milk  that a cow produces.
a. Use supply and demand analysis to explain the effect on the milk market.
b. Explain how milk consumers would be affected.
Suppose the hormone doubles the quantity of milk that would supplied at any given price.
c. Show how much the supply curve shifts.
d. T/F -- Explain -- "the quantity of milk exchanged double."

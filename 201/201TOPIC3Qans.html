Some discussion of Topic 3's  Problems for Practice and Review

 Suppose there is news that contrary to popular belief, eating carrots does not help a person's eyesight.
a. Use supply and demand analysis to explain how the carrot market would be affected.

Suggestion:   the supply would increase, which causes the price to decrease, and quantity to increase right?

Response:  No, demand would decrease. That reduces both price and quantity; of course you need to show it on the graph. Think about it and you'll see that it doesn't make sense for the quantity to increase here. People won't want carrots so much, and so fewer carrots will be bought and sold in the market.

Note that an answer to the question requires that you draw the graph.

 Suppose there is news that contrary to popular belief, eating carrots does not help a person's eyesight.
 b  Explain how carrot producers would be affected.

Student question: “Would it be plausible to state that you would need more information because the producer of carrots would have a lower price and a lower quantity but production costs would also decrease because they don't have to spend as much to reach the higher quantity? (or for example they could sell a farm, or save money on farming equipment/employees).”

 No, we don't need more information -- the reduction is Q does decrease production costs, but the reduction in benefit is greater and so the producer of carrots will be worse off.  Keep in mind that suppliers were gaining from supplying the carrots ...

 

b  Explain how carrot producers would be affected.
 

Comment: draw the model to show that P and Q both decrease. Carrot producers will be worse off (hurt) by both the lower price and the lower quantity.

Optional discussion: A level students can relate this result to the gains from trade model. They will see that the decline in price gives more of the gains from trade to the buyer, and less of the gains from trade to the seller (i.e. the price drop redistributes gains from trade to the buyer from the seller). And they will see that the decline in quantity reduces the gains from trade. On both counts suppliers' gains from trade are reduced. 

In microeconomics you will learn how to show the sellers losses/gains in the S&D model (areas called "producer surplus").

 

 Suppose milk producers adopt a synthetic growth hormone that significantly increases the amount of milk  that a cow produces.
a. Use supply and demand analysis to explain the effect on the milk market.
b. Explain how milk consumers would be affected.

Suppose the hormone doubles the quantity of milk that would supplied at any given price.
c. Show how much the supply curve shifts.
d. T/F -- Explain -- "the quantity of milk exchanged double."

Suggestion:   the new supply curve would be two times further to the right. If the price stayed the same then quantity would double, but with a normal looking demand curve the new equilibrium will have a lower price and the quantity will be less than double.

Response:  Correct!  Be sure to show that in the S&D graph.

 

Suppose an increase in the cost of labor (wages) increases the costs of restaurant meals in Mt. Pleasant by 5 %.
a. Show what will happen to the supply of restaurant meals.


b. Use supply and demand analysis to explain what will happen to market for restaurant meals.  Will the price of meals go up by 5 % ?

Question: how do we use the 5% information?  

Answer: the height of the supply curve will be 5% greater (supply shifts upward 5%).

 

Explain how cherry farmers are affected if there is bad weather that damages the cherry crop.
 

Comment: decreased Supply will reduce quantity and increase price. Reduced quantity is bad for the farmers but increased price is good for them. Bad weather could be good news (if price increases enough).

TOPIC 3: Supply and Demand

Supply and Demand is the model of a competitive market, meaning that buyers and sellers are price-takers.  

A market (for something) is an arrangement for buying and selling (something).  A competitive market is a market in which buyers and sellers are price-takers.

Price-taking

A price-taker responds to the market price (because s/he/it does not affect the market price; a price-taking agent's individual action has no effect on the market price).

A price-taking seller is called a supplier. A supplier sells in response to the market price and call sell any desired quantity at that price. It can't charge more than the market price because ________________.

A price-taking buyer is called a demander. A demander buys in response to the market price, and can buy any desired quantity at the market price.  It can't pay less than the market price because _____________.

Discussion: A buyer whose individual choice affects price is called a price-maker and the simplest example is the monopsonist: the sole buyer in a market.  Monopsony power is the ability of a buyer to reduce price by reducing the amount bought.  A monopsonist restricts purchases, resulting in a lower price and lower quantity exchanged compared to a competitive market.  Possible examples of buyers with significant monopsony power include the University as a buyer of on-campus student labor, and an automobile factory in a small isolated town (but workers can move, which limits the factory's power to depress wages). These are cases where the buyer may have some perceptible effect on the price (wage) they pay.  For ECO 201 you can leave the concept of price-maker in the background, but if you go on in economics you will see it again.


Demand

Demand is the model of price-taking buyers. A demander buys in response to the market price, and can buy any desired quantity at the market price. Because competing buyers are  offering to pay the market price, the demander cannot get the good at a lower price. 

Qd is quantity-demanded; it is the amount that a buyer (or buyers) would be willing (and able) to purchase in response to a specified price. Quantity is usually a flow variable, for example gallons of gas per month. 

Demand is the relationship between Qd and price. Keep in mind demand is not an amount, so don't use the term "demand" when you mean "quantity-demanded."

Law of Demand: the Demand curve has negative slope.  In words: ceterus paribus, quantity-demanded is inversely (negatively) affected by price (e.g. if price increases, Qd decreases; if price decreases, Qd increases).

1. substitution effect: when the price of a good increases, other choices become relatively more attractive. 

2. income effect: when the price of a good increases, it becomes less affordable.

Supply

Supply is the model of price-taking sellers. A supplier sells in response to the market price and call sell any desired quantity at that price. Because competing sellers are offering the good at the market price, a supplier cannot charge a higher price.

Discussion: A seller whose individual choice affects price is called a price-maker and the simplest example is the monopolist: the sole seller in a market.  Monopoly power is the ability of a seller to increase price by reducing the amount sold. A monopolist restricts sales, resulting in a higher price and lower quantity-exchanged compared to a competitive market.  Possible examples of sellers with significant monopoly power include the University as a seller of credit hours and a seller of dorm-housing, the power company, and a pharmaceutical company with a patented drug. These are cases where the seller may have some perceptible effect on the price they receive.  For ECO 201 you can leave the concept of price-maker in the background, but if you go on in economics you will see it again.

0. Equilibrium, when the market price balances S & D  (Qs=Qd)

1. Adjustments to equilibrium

Excess demand (roughly, a shortage) causes price to _____, this causes Qs to ___ and Qd ___ so the excess demand ___.  There will be excess demand if price is too ____ .

Excess supply (roughly, a surplus) cause price to  ____, this causes Qs to ___ and Qd ___ so the excess  supply ___ .   There will be excess supply if price is too ____.

When a market works well, price adjusts instantaneously to keep shifting supplies and demands in balance. If price is slow to adjust we may observe periods with either a shortage or a surplus in a market. When there is a shortage of a good the price is too low; when there is a surplus of a good the price is too high.  In the basic supply and demand model, the market works well, so price adjusts quickly to keep demand and supply in balance (equilibrium).

6.3  sources of growth ?

TEQ: what are the two basic sources of economic growth

growth of per capita output comes from
1) growth of technology (knowledge), aka technical change or innovation.
2) growth of resources (L,K,T) per person (or growth of per capita resources).

why not per capita technology?                                  (non-rivalness)


More L, K, T, is more input, and more input yields more output. More resources per person (more input per person) produces more output per person.
Growth of technology is more complex; it enables us to produce more in various different ways, sometimes summarized as increased productivity.
--  new/better outputs ... medicines and medical care, smart phones & computers, hair gel, etc.
 -- new/better inputs ... e.g. materials (stone, wood, metal, glass, rubber, synthetics); energy sources (sun, wood, wind, coal, oil, hydro, nuclear, solar);  tools, equipment, machinery, buildings  etc.
-- new/better ways to organize production or society ... factory, assembly line, schools, organizations and institutions of the society: business organizations, legal structures, ......all in all -- more output.

Productivity: the ratio of output to input (not to be confused with efficiency, which refers to a lack of waste). The textbook focusses on labor productivity. In class we focus on the productivity of resources more generally.

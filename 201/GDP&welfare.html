2. Distribution


Looking at income distribution and per capita GDP:
                                         (Slovak Rep)              (Brazil)              Compare A to  B
                                           Economy A            Economy B
pcGDP                                  $6000                    $9000               A has 2/3 the pcGDP
share to rich (1/5)                     40%                       70%                    

share to the rest                        60%                       30%               A's non-rich get twice the share of B's 

to compare how much the non-rich 4/5 get, we simply multiply the comparisons of pcGDP and of shares -- here that is 2/3 * 2/1 = 4/3

so, other things equal, the non-rich in economy A get 4/3  (133%)  as much as in economy B.  The A people live in a poorer economy (2/3 as rich),  but  most of them  (the non-rich 4/5) get twice the share of production that the non-rich of B get -- which combines to the 4/3 (=1.33=133%).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
To confirm this, consider the GDP per person for the non-rich (4/5 of households) ... 
for Economy A:   [0.60*(6000*popA)]/[0.80*popA]
for Economy B:   [0.30*(9000*popB)]/ [0.80*popB]

To compare these, take the ratio:  (0.6/0.3)*(6000/9000)=2*2/3=4/3=1.33

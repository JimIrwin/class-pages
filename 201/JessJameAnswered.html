Given time-costs,

Food Clothingcost of clothing
Jamie 2 hrs/loaf 20 hrs/suit10 loaves per suit
Jesse 5 hrs/loaf 30 hrs/suit 6  loaves per suit

If Jamie produces a suit, she gives up the chance to make 10 loaves    [Jamie takes 20 hrs to make a suit; in 2 hrs she can make one loaf, so in 20 hours she could make 10 loaves]

If Jesse produces a suit, he gives up the chance to make 6 loaves.

Since Jesse has the lower cost of suits ( 6 < 10 ),  he has the comparative advantage in suits; and so Jamie has the comparative advantage in food.


Gainful trade


They gain from trade if Jesse makes a suit for Jamie, in exchange for food.

Jesse  gains if he gets more than  6  loaves (getting paid more then the cost)

Jamie  gains if she pays less than  10  loaves (paying less then what the suit is worth)    [It would cost Jamie 10 loaves to make a suit, so a suit is worth 10 loaves to Jamie (the benefit of a suit is 10L) ]

And so Both  gain if  Jamie  pays Jesse between 6 and 10 loaves per suit.

For example, both can have a gain worth 2 loaves if the price is  8  loaves per suit.

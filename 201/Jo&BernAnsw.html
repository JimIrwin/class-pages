Given time-costs,

cleaning paintingcost of painting
Jo 1 hr/apt 5 hrs/apt5 cl per painting
Bernie 3hrs/apt 9 hrs/apt3 cl per painting

If Jo paints an apartment, she gives up the chance to clean 5  apartments (in the 5 hrs it takes her to paint, she could clean 5 apts).

If Bernie paints an apartment, he gives up the chance to clean 3 apartments (in the 9 hrs it takes him to paint, he could clean 3 apts).

Since _Bernie___ has the lower cost of painting ( _3_  <  _5_ ), he has the comparative advantage in painting; and so  _Jo___ has the comparative advantage in cleaning.


Gainful trade

They gain from trade if _Bernie_ paints an apartment for _Jo___, in exchange for cleanings.

_Bernie_  gains if he gets more than _3_ cleanings

_Jo__  gains if she pays less than _5_ cleanings.

Both gain if _Jo___ pays _Bernie____ between _3__ and _5__ cleanings per paint-job.

For example, both can have a gain worth 1 cleaning if the price is _4__ cleanings per paint-job.

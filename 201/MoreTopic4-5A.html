Topic 4 (Output)

 

1. Given the following information for an hypothetical economy:


year        production                prices per
          bikes    taxi-rides        bike        taxi-ride
1        10              50                 $100     $5
2        11              60                   $80     $4
3          9              80                 $120     $6
4        12              40                   $50     $5 

a. Compare outputs in year 1 and year 2.
year 1 real ouput:         1250 $1           1000 $2

year 2 real ouput:         1400 $1          1120 $2

 

Year 2 output is 112% of year 1 output (1400/1250=1120/1000=112%). No INP because relative prices were constant (pure inflation).
 

 

b. Compare outputs in year 2 and year 3.
                           

year 2 real ouput:           1120 $2       1680 $3

year 3  real ouput:         1040 $2        1560 $3

 

Year 3 output is about 93% of year 2 output (1040/1120=1560/1680=98.9%). No INP because relative prices were constant (pure inflation).
 


c. T/F ? --explain-- output was greater in year 4 than in year 3.
                                    year 3$         year 4$
year 3 real output:         1560            850
year 4 real output:         1680            800

Other ...  It is true if we use year 3 as the base (1680>1560), but not if we use year 4 as the base (800<850). There is an index number problem because relative prices changed (impure inflation). [Note that the bike price drops much more, so using year 4 as base makes bikes less important to output ...]

 

2. Given the following information for an hypothetical economy:
year        production                prices per
               suits        bread            suits         loaf of bread
0            20            200                    $8            $ 1
1            30            100                    $20          $ 4

a. Compare outputs of the two years.
                                    year 0$         year 1$
year 0 real ouput:         360            1200
year 1 real ouput:         340            1000
comparison:                 0.944           0.833

Based on year 0 prices, Year 1 output was about  6% less than year 0 output; but based on year 1 prices, it was about 17 % less.
Both comparisons are correct -- there is not one right comparison because relative prices changed (inflation was impure)..
 

b. Compare the average level of prices of the two years. Does it matter which is the base year ?
Base year 0: prices in year 1 were 353 % of prices in year 0. (1200/360=3.3333)
Base year 1: prices in year 1 were 294 % of prices in year 0. (1000/340=2.941...)
It does matter which is the base year, but not a lot (294% is not too far from 333%)

c. T/F/ Explain: "inflation was less for nudists than for people who wear suits."
False: inflation would be greater for nudists, because the price of food grew more than the price of suits did.  The more that a person spent on suits the lower her inflation would be.

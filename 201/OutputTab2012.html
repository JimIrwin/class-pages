"real output," output measured in terms of goods, using base-year dollars or some other fixed standard of value.  This is the theory of correcting for inflation. There are two approaches to measuring real output: (1) the productions of different times or places are valued using a common set of prices; (2) a price index is used to convert from nominal output to real output.

* bottom line: from 2005 to 2006 there was impure inflation, so people experienced different inflations, depending on the relative importance of the goods. Soda pop prices rose more, and as a result Lucy, who spends more of her snack budget on soda pop, saw a greater increase in the cost of her snacks (compared to Jill & Homer, or to Fred).

A level bonus material: the choice of base year can create additional index number problems; suppose that in 2006  Lucy buys 1 HD and 1.5 SP per game (reducing Qd of SP because of the increased relative price).  In the 3 years, that bundle of goods (1 HD  1.5 SP) would cost $3.50   $5.25  and $11; the implied price indexes would be 100 150 314   (11/3.5 ~= 314). So Lucy's inflation looks lower from the vantage point of 2006 than it does from the vantage point of 2004

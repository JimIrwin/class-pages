4 .1  Total Production & the Price Level in Theory

The economy exists to produce the goods that people need and want to live and live well.  The first key macroeconomic question: how much is produced?  The answer is called the economy's "output."  

Output, y, the economy's total output. "y" == is the total amount of final goods produced (in a period of time, usually a year).

final goods -- at the end of production-- as opposed to intermediate goods. Final as in the "final" final user had the product. Output excludes intermediate goods, which are goods used in producing other goods.

3 Key Macro Math Tools to know:

1.  "compare" using a ratio (division, not subtraction)

2. Take the LN of the ratio to find growth. 

3. Divide growth (g) by the number of years (t) to find average annual growth (ag).  ag=g/t

The rule of 69.3% for doubling (because LN(2) ~ 0.693).   For example, growth that averages 7% per year results in a doubling in just under 10 years (10*7=70% and 69.3% growth is double).  Annual growth of 4% results in a doubling in just over 17 years (4*17=68).   [formula:  ag=g/t  so  g=ag*t   and  t = g/ag]

Note that when growth is not large (say, 20% or less), then growth and percentage change are about the same (for example, Ln(1.2) is about 0.18 (a change of 20% is a growth of 18% ).

Note that that when numbers double or triple in value, percentage changes can be very misleading.


Two-Good Model

aggregation

nominal output: the money-value of output; here output is expressed in terms of the current money of that economy (so year 2's output is expressed in terms of year 2's money). The general point to recall is that "nominal" means "in terms of money" and "real" means  "in terms of goods," where good is some fixed standard of value (such as a good or bundle of goods, or the dollars of a specified time or place."

real output:  output measured in terms of a specified standard of value, such as base-year dollars.  Thus output calculated using the prices of a specified time and place. Our Real Output concept is also called "constant dollar output" or "constant price output."

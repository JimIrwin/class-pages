Video assignment #1 (for week 1) : watch this orientation video from the AEA; for test #1 be prepared to answer simple questions to demonstrate that you paid attention (background info).        

Reading assignment #1 (for week 1).  Alfred Marshall, Principles of Economics (1890-1912), Book 1, Chapter 1, Sections 1-2 (other formats LEL M). This brief item provides perspective on economics as a scholarly field; for test 1, you'll be expected to recognize major points that Marshall covers. 

Reading assignment #2 (for week 2). UMN: Chapter 1, Appendix A; OpenStax, Chapter 1 and The Use of Mathematics in Principles of Economics. Look over the materials for orientation, then for test 1, focus on the following: economics, scarcity, choice, cost, maximization (not important in 201), choices at the margin, micro vs macro, scientific method, models (theory), ceterus paribus, false cause, normative vs positive.   The appendix materials on graphing should be review; if you have questions or concerns about the appendix material, raise them in class or on Piazza.

from the syllabus: 


 Optional Reading Report, replacing your lowest 21 points from tests or exams.

The report will summarize and discuss an academic journal article (or part of a scholarly book) dealing with some aspect of US economic history. 
3  points: your selection (by email) of an approved reading, based on your web search, in consultation with Dr. Irwin;
6  points: your descriptive summary of the reading, in at most 1 page in a form of your choice (sentences required);
12 points: your reflective essay (5-6 pages) discussing the reading in relation to ECO 310.

___________
DEADLINES:   Optional reading report: reading selection due Fri 2/17; summary due Thu 3/16; reflective essay due Thu 4

Guidelines:

In your "selection"  assignment, you earn 3 points by getting approved for a scholarly article (or book chapter)  that covers some topic or issue in US economic history (1492-2015), based on your interests. Your approved reading can be either an article in a scholarly journal (such as Journal of Economic History  or American Historical Review)  or  one or more chapters from a scholarly book; the reading must fall under the heading of US economic history.  To complete the selection assigment, you email Prof. Irwin requesting a specific reading, with a brief explanation of what the article covers and why you chose it. Check the bibliographys (references) of the readings on the Active Course Outline. For a broader range of topics,   Google scholar will useful, and more general web searches might also work; CMU's library may have other useful resources.

In the one page summary the goal is to provide an in-depth description of your reading, providing lots of information about what the piece is about (issues, evidence, methods, findings).  The challenge here is to be informative despite the short space.  In part you will be demonstrating that you did the reading, but mostly you will be producing valuable information: a one page guide to your reading.  Of course the summary will be in your own words except for quotations. The one page limit is strict, the format is flexible (complete sentences are required, paragraph structure recommended).

In the 5-6 page essay the goal is to draw out connections between the reading and concepts and issues and topics that we cover in ECO 310.  In part, you will be demonstrating that the essay is your own product (it would be difficult to buy an internet essay that discussed both the reading and our class!), but mostly you will be producing a valuable good: your reflections on connections between the reading and the course.  The 6 page limitation is not strict; it is ok if you need more space to develop your insights (but unnecessary length would pull down the score).

* Academic honesty issues: 1) DISCLOSURE: let me know if you are covering the reading for another class so we can avoid double-counting.  2) PLAIGARISM: be careful to use quotation marks when you use other people's writing and to use references for ideas and facts (parenthetical or footnote or endnote).  Avoid paraphrasing; use quotations instead.

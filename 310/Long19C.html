 Slavery-Era Insurance Policies  A fascinating report to the California Legislature in 2002. The posting student highlights the following passage:  "New York Life reported that of the first 1,000 policies written by the predecessor, 339 were on the lives of slaves and that these policies were usually written for less than $500 and for a term of one year. New York Life reported that there were three death claims under slave policies in the period under review, with a total of $1,050 paid."
(Nautilus Insurance was the predecessor of NY Life; it started selling life insurance policies in 1845, including policies for slaves until 1848. Note that over 1/3 of  the first 1,000 policies were for slaves' lives. Note that the NY Times article describes the Board of Trustees; the CA report refers to that Board's decision to stop dealing with slave life insurance in 1848).

1b. Effects:

The current mainstream view is that per capita output fell during the war, and did not recover until the later 1790s. This is based mainly on export data, but Virginia probate data casts doubt on the conventional wisdom. As well as *Economic Impact of Independence see the evidence on *land prices in VA & PA and more(McDevitt & Irwin, 2014/20)

 Slavery-Era Insurance Policies  A fascinating report to the California Legislature in 2002. The student who sent it highlighted the following passage: "New York Life reported that of the first 1,000 policies written by the predecessor, 339 were on the lives of slaves and that these policies were usually written for less than $500 and for a term of one year.

Indentured Servants:

*Over ½ of the Euros to the US colonies came as servants.

* In exchange for passage to a specified colony, they signed to serve a specified term of labor (time to serve).


* Basic economics: sale of servant's contract will pay for the passage cost. How may years of work it would take to repay that cost determines the length of service, so it depends on the value of labor, thus on characteristics of individuals and destinations    : 
-- individuals: age/sex/skill/literacy
-- destination: shortest in West Indies longest in New England (few there)

* 17th C English to Chesapeake (headright system -- buyer also gets land)
   18th C Irish, Scots, Germans to Mid-Atlantic colonies

> 3/4 male, 15-25 years old; 4-7 year terms (unbalanced sex ratio, impact on intial population growth)..

* temporarily a slave ... worse or better living conditions than slaves? incentives versus culture ?
– incentives: conditions of a worker now affect their well-being in 10 years and beyond; does owner care about what worker will be able to do in 20 years ? IS or slave at age 40
– culture? slaves as “other” or outsider – easier to ignore their humanity and abuse them? E.g. slave women work in the fields, just like the men. < ¼ IS were women, and they were usually not assigned field work … ?   Role of cultures from Africa vs  Europe.

* contract labor is  "voluntary" -- but waves depend on English conditions.  In the 19th C, people from India "voluntarily" become the labor force for the 2nd plantation system, escaping famine in India ...

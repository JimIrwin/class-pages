1b. Amerindian Population Collapse (AIPC)

Estimated levels of aboriginal populations of the "New World", selected years:
circa 1490 50 to 75 million (or more ?)
circa 1650 about 5 million
circa 1700 about 1 million
circa 1980 about 25 million (and some 90 million "mestizo," or mixed ancestry, part aboriginal).
 

 Discussion here of the very dramatic decrease in the aboriginal populations of the Americas (sometimes described as the "Amerindian Holocaust")

  - normative issues of naming:  genocide? holocaust? unlucky?

  - numbers debate ("high counters?");  uniqueness (have historians understated the European population declines of the Black Death (1348-54))?

  - exogenous or endogenous? (causes of death;   effects of war, slavery (mining), missions; intentions?)

The Trans-Atlantic Slave Trade

 "In the US, African and European populations had about the same demographic experiences." But what about 12.8 > 4.7 ?

Here's a simple model to illustrate that a difference in the timing of arrivals can explain the differing demographic experience ratios for slave and free "migrants" to North America.
Suppose there equal numbers of European-free and African-slave arrivals (40,000); but the Euros arrived in 1630 and the Afros arrived in 1690.  With populations doubling every 30 years, we see the Euro head start gives them a much bigger  ratio of population to arrivals.  So we have very different population-arrival ratios with  the same demographic experiences (population doubling every 30 years) .
European-free  population:  1630: 40      1660: 80     1690: 160      1720: 320   demog.  ratio 8
African-slave  population:                                           1690   40      1720   80    demog.  ratio 2 (actually, of the 390K slaves arriving in the US, only 15K arrived before 1700).
